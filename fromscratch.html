My Dance Journey

About My Dance Life

I started dancing at age 3 and competed through age 18. 15 years spent training, competing, and growing as a dancer. From tiny recitals to regional competitions, I would say dance shaped who I am, teaching me discipline, teamwork, creativity, and how to perform under pressure. I trained and competed in multiple styles, traveled across the country for competitions, and had the honor of leading my high school dance team as captain for two of my four years on the team.

Dance Styles

Over the years I trained and competed in a variety of styles, each one teaching me something different:

  1. Hip Hop
    • High energy, rhythm driven choreography
    • Freestyle elements
    • One of my favorite competition styles
  2. Jazz
    • Sharp, precise movements
    • Emphasis on technique and performance
  3. Contemporary
    • Expressive, fluid movements
    • Focus on storytelling and emotional expression
  4. Ballet
    • The foundation of all my other training
    • Classical technique, barre work, and pointe training

High School Dance Team

I was a member of my high school dance team for all four years. During my junior and senior years, I served as a team captain. It was an experience that pushed me to grow as both a dancer and a leader.

  • 4 years on the team
  • 2 years as captain


As captain, I helped plan practices, mentor newer dancers, coordinate with coaches, and lead the team before performances. It taught me what it truly means to show up for your team.
